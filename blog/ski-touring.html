Ski Touring in the Sharr: Europe's Hidden Powder Paradise

February 28, 2026 · 6 min read

Every winter, while skiers queue for overcrowded lifts in the Alps, the Sharr Mountains sit quietly under meters of untouched powder. No lift lines. No crowds. No groomed runs. Just pure, raw ski touring in one of Europe's last truly wild mountain ranges.

The Snow

The Sharr Mountains receive consistent snowfall from December through March. Continental influence brings cold, dry powder — the kind that makes you whoop with every turn. At higher elevations, good snow conditions can last well into April.

The Terrain

Think wide-open bowls above treeline, perfectly spaced birch forests for tree runs, and couloirs for those seeking steeper challenges. The elevation range of 1,500m to 2,700m offers incredible variety in a compact area.

Why It's Special

In a word: solitude. You can skin up for an hour and have an entire mountain face to yourself. No helicopters, no cat tracks, no other groups. Just you, your guide, and endless untracked snow.

"Three days of touring and we didn't see another skier. Fresh tracks every single run. This place is ten years behind the Alps — in the best possible way." — Andreas, Austria

Our Winter Package

Our ski touring package includes experienced mountain guides with avalanche training, all safety equipment (beacon, probe, shovel), comfortable accommodation, and warming meals after cold days on the mountain. We also offer snowshoe excursions for non-skiers.
